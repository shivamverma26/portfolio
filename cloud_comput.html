Cloud Computing 101: What You Need to Know.

If you're new to the world of cloud computing, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. But with more and more businesses moving their data to the cloud, it's becoming increasingly important to understand this technology.

"Cloud computing is not only the future of computing, but the present and the entire past of computing." - Larry Ellison

Although this quote emphasizes the idea that cloud computing is not just the future, but it has always been a part of computing's history. This suggests that cloud computing has always been an integral part of how we store, manage, and access data, even before the term "cloud" was coined.

At its most basic level, cloud computing refers to the practice of using remote servers to store, manage, and process data instead of using local servers or personal computers. This means that instead of having to manage your own hardware and software, you can use cloud services to access computing power and storage on demand.


One of the biggest benefits of cloud computing is scalability. Because you're not limited by the resources of your own hardware, you can easily scale up or down as needed to meet the demands of your business. This makes it a popular choice for businesses that have fluctuating workloads or that need to quickly ramp up their computing power for short-term projects.

Another benefit of cloud computing is cost savings. By outsourcing your computing needs to a cloud provider, you can avoid the expense of maintaining your own hardware and software. Instead, you pay for only what you use, which can be much more cost-effective in the long run.

There are three main types of cloud computing: public, private, and hybrid. Public cloud services are provided by third-party providers like Amazon Web Services or Microsoft Azure and are accessible to anyone with an internet connection. Private cloud services, on the other hand, are hosted on dedicated infrastructure that is only accessible to a single organization. Hybrid cloud services combine elements of both public and private cloud services.
In conclusion, Cloud computing is a powerful and cost-effective technology that enables businesses to scale, save costs, and access a range of tools and services. Whether you're new to cloud or looking to expand, it's worth exploring its possibilities.
